Planning Your Day

An important part to spending your day efficiently is knowing what you are going to be doing that day.

Without a plan you will likely waste a lot of time doing tasks that are not important or spending time on apps like instagram or tiktok.

You will often find that if you are spending your time wisely, on things that are important you will have more time to do the things you want to do, because truthfully there is a lot of time in a day or in a week but people waste a lot of their time.

Morning Routine

A good morning routine will help kickstart your day and get you motivated and moving which will set yourself up for great success.

A bad routine will either have you worn out too early in the morning and have you wasting too much time or leave you unmotivated or lethargic in the morning.

Exercise

There are many studies proving that exercise at some point in the day is very good for mental as well as physical health.

Exercise, such as cardio or resistance training will not only boost your baseline dopamine level through the day but will also leave you feeling energized and more productive.

Along with those benefits, therea re the obvious bonuses of helping increase physical health, such as strength.

The Pomodoro Technique
  1. Pick a task
  2. Set a 25 minute timer
  3. Work until the timer runs out
  4. Take a 5 minute break
  5. Repeat steps 1-4 three more times
  6. After 2 hours take a longer, 15 minute break
  7. Repeat the process
Free Time

It is always important to spend time with yourself, doing things you enjoy.

This can be anything and will boost mental health as well as reduce the risk of burnout.
